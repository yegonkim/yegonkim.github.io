I wanted to read some other theoretical papers on out-of-context reasoning [[1]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.08218)[[2]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.10887) before writing this post, but I figured I would never get around to it if I kept waiting. So here I present my rough thoughts on why OOCR isn't so mysterious when you look at it through the lens of algorithmic information theory.

***

A year ago I was watching [this episode](https://youtu.be/64lXQP6cs5M?t=2093) from Dwarkesh podcast, and around 34:53-35:44 Dwarkesh expresses his surprise that a large language model can learn to use documents, seen only during its supervised fine-tuning phase, in a way that resembles "conscious logical deduction", that is, as if the documents were part of the context.
